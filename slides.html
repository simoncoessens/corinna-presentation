Corinna Legal Background

The DSA Architecture

Regulation (EU) 2022/2065 — A comprehensive framework for digital intermediaries

Territorial Scope — “Substantial Connection”

ART. 2(1) Applies to intermediary services offered to recipients in the EU, irrespective of where providers are established.

Substantial Connection to the EU Art. 3(e) • Recital 8
Path A
Establishment in the EU
Physical presence in a Member State — registered office, branch, or subsidiary.
OR
Path B — In absence of establishment
Factual Criteria
Significant number of recipients
In one or more Member States, relative to population
OR
Targeting of activities
Towards one or more Member States
Targeting indicators (Recital 8)
Language / Currency Use of a language or currency generally used in a Member State
Products & TLD Ordering of products/services; use of a relevant top-level domain (.de, .fr…)
App store availability App available in a national application store
Local advertising Advertising in local language or directed at local consumers
Brussels I bis Directing activities per Reg. 1215/2012, Art. 17(1)(c)
× Mere technical accessibility of a website does not establish substantial connection Recital 8

Intermediary Services — Classification & Obligations

ALL INTERMEDIARY SERVICES Arts. 11–15 Points of contact, legal representative, T&Cs, transparency reports
Mere Conduit Art. 4

Transmit info or provide network access. No selection, no modification.

Caching Art. 5

Automatic temporary storage for efficient onward transmission.

HOSTING Arts. 16–18 Notice & action mechanisms, statement of reasons
ONLINE PLATFORMS Arts. 20–28 Complaints, dispute settlement, trusted flaggers
Online Marketplaces Arts. 29–32

Trader traceability (Art. 30), compliance by design (Art. 31), right to information (Art. 32)

General platforms

Store & disseminate to the public at request of recipient

Online Search Engines Art. 4(j)

Index & search all websites based on queries in any format

Micro / Small Arts. 19, 29

Exempt from platform & marketplace obligations. 12-month grace period. Overridden if designated VLOP.

VLOP Arts. 33–43

Very Large Online Platforms (45M+ monthly users). Risk assessment, systemic risk mitigation, audits, data access, crisis protocols.

VLOSE Arts. 33–43

Very Large Online Search Engines (45M+ monthly users). Same enhanced obligations.

Thesis

LLMs as a Regulatory Compliance Advisor

From commentary writing to compliance assessment

1

Engel & Kruse (2025) show LLMs can produce structured legal commentaries when the doctrinal structure is provided externally — not discovered by the model.

2

The same logic applies to regulatory compliance: encode the regulation’s structure into the pipeline and let the LLM fill in the substance. The solution must be resilient to prompt sensitivity — prompts need to be tested and refined by domain experts.

3

Every output is traceable to specific articles, and the user validates or overrides at each step — the LLM never acts alone.

4

Key advantage is timing: a structured first draft in minutes, not weeks — helping laypeople understand the legal framework. The tool must be easily updatable as law, case law, and guidelines evolve.

5

The user must be able to interact and ask questions — and the tool needs to respond with factual accuracy, grounded in the actual legal sources.

Deep Research — q14
q14

Does this company operate an online platform?

A service that stores and disseminates information to the public at the request of a recipient

Legal Brief provided to agent

An online platform is a hosting service that stores and disseminates information to the public at the request of a recipient of the service.

The key legal distinction is dissemination — making information available to a potentially unlimited number of third parties.

Exclusions:

Comment sections (ancillary feature), cloud storage (minor), private messaging (finite recipients)

VLOP threshold:

≥45 million monthly active EU users
