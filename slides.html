Corinna Thesis

LLMs as a Regulatory Compliance Advisor

From commentary writing to compliance assessment (Engel & Kruse 2025)

1

Engel & Kruse (2025) show LLMs can produce structured legal commentaries when the doctrinal structure is provided externally — not discovered by the model.

2

Same logic applies to regulatory compliance: Corinna encodes the DSA’s structure into the pipeline and lets the LLM fill in the substance.

3

Every output is traceable to specific DSA articles, and the user validates or overrides at each step — the LLM never acts alone.

4

Key advantage is timing: a structured first draft in minutes, not weeks — making compliance accessible to SMEs.

Corinna Academic Context

Theoretical Framework

The evolution of Legal AI: from static analysis to agentic reasoning.

MACHINE LEARNING Lippi et al.

CLAUDETTE: Detecting Unfair Clauses

  • Used SVMs and CNNs to detect unfair clauses in Terms of Service
  • Static classification approach — pattern matching on contract language
  • Limited to pre-trained categories, no reasoning about novel provisions

Pioneering work in legal AI — but constrained by the ML paradigm of its time.

LLM CAPABILITIES Engel & Kruse 2025

LLMs as Legal Experts

  • LLMs can summarize jurisprudence and generate commentaries comparable to human experts
  • Key requirement: a structured pipeline to guide the model
  • Enables statutory reasoning and subsumption (applying facts to rules)

“We do not let the structure guiding the analysis emerge from the case law, but externally provide this expert knowledge to the LLM.”

OUR APPROACH

Corinna: Agentic Compliance

  • Applies LLM generative capability to regulatory compliance
  • Unlike CLAUDETTE’s static ML, uses LLMs for statutory reasoning & subsumption
  • Wraps the LLM in a structured multi-agent pipeline — architecture enforces the legal logic

Corinna Interdisciplinarity

The “Tech Lawyer” & the Translation Problem

After Guadamuz (2025)

The Translation Crisis
Courts decide AI’s fate via metaphors: is a training set a “mixtape”? A “compressed database”? A “synthetic brain”?
Lawyers rely on expert witnesses who struggle to connect technical explanations to legal elements
Diverging rulings on the same tech: Getty Images (London) vs. GEMA (Munich) — opposite conclusions on whether models “store” data
The Tech Lawyer: Dual Fluency
Parse a dataset as easily as a contract
Understand the architecture of a neural network and of a legal argument
Know which simplifications are permissible vs. misleading

“This kind of translation is an art form … requires a deep enough understanding of the tech to know which simplifications are permissible and which are misleading.”

— Guadamuz, 2025

CONNECTION
1

Engel & Kruse’s pipeline requires a tech lawyer: the LLM cannot infer doctrine alone — someone must teach it the doctrinal framework through prompts

2

Corinna embodies the same logic: dual fluency designs the agent pipeline, encodes the DSA’s structure, crafts the classification logic

The tech lawyer is not replaced — the tech lawyer is the architect
SCALABILITY
Individual expertise

rare, high-value

Reusable pipeline

scalable, updatable

Encode the tech lawyer’s expertise into a tool: compliance assessment run by a non-specialist, commentary updated automatically. Knowledge multiplied, not replaced.

Inside a Research Agent

Deep Research — q14
q14

Does this company operate an online platform?

A service that stores and disseminates information to the public at the request of a recipient

Legal Brief provided to agent

An online platform is a hosting service that stores and disseminates information to the public at the request of a recipient of the service.

The key legal distinction is dissemination — making information available to a potentially unlimited number of third parties.

Exclusions:

Comment sections (ancillary feature), cloud storage (minor), private messaging (finite recipients)

VLOP threshold:

≥45 million monthly active EU users
